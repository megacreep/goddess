Guiding Question

As we all know, life at CMU is very busy and full of hard work. Most of our time is either at home or campus. Also, a lot of people stay late and leave the campus at midnight, which is very unhealthy. We all want to change our lifestyle to a healthy pattern. However, we may not be aware of our daily life and find excuses for ourselves as "It will be the last time I go home this late." or "I will go out and relax a little bit this weekend." In most cases, it is no more than a placebo. So, in this project byte, I want to collect my location data for the whole month and monitor my daily activities. I hope to know my "footprints" in Pittsburgh in terms of sequential geometry information, accumulated data, and data segments by the time period during the day.

2

Data Quality

Completeness

I think the data completeness is fairly good. I accessed my dataset by looking into how much data points I have collected per day and their time distribution.

The biggest potential problem that will result in incomplete data is the failure in data collection. To improve the data completeness, I charged my phone regularly and put it in my pocket all the time. However, there may still be some missing value. For example, my phone died when I was snowboarding due to the cold weather. Also, that day is not representative since I only went to the resort once this year. Thus, to rule out the possibility that these missing data will affect my results, I deleted that day while plotting an accumulated location map (heat map).

Coherence

I think the data coherence is good. The data made sense to me since it is fairly represented my daily travel activity and met my expectations of my typical days in CMU. Also, the data points distribution is relevant to the places I spent most of my time. One exception is Feb 4th; there are many outliers on that day. However, since I traveled to a snowboarding resort, the data make sense to me.

Correctness

The dataset is fairly correct. I manually verified the data in two ways. Before the study, I collected a test data sample both indoor and outdoor in 5 locations. I spent two hours at each location and checked the data points I collected during the meantime. The result showed that the offset is less than 20 meters and the timestamp matched the time I conducted these tests. I believe it is high enough for this study and there is no need for calibration. Moreover, to ensure that the accuracy is stable over time, I compared the data points I collected during 3 am to 7 am for the first five days. The data showed that I was at home during all these time periods. The results are in agreement with the facts.

Accountability

I collect the data with AWARE from my phone 6s GPS sensor. Since iphone5, Apple has been using A-GPS, GLONASS, and Wi-Fi positioning system to provide user location information, which is fairly accurate with less than 10m offset outdoor. I believe the accuracy of this data is high enough for this study purpose.
